Child Custody in Oregon

Understanding how custody decisions are made

Best Interests of the Child

Oregon courts make custody decisions based on the "best interests of the child" standard. This means the court considers:

  • The child's emotional ties with each parent
  • Each parent's interest in and attitude toward the child
  • The desirability of continuing existing relationships
  • Abuse of one parent by the other
  • The preference of the child (if age-appropriate)
  • The willingness and ability of each parent to facilitate a close relationship between the child and the other parent

Types of Custody

Legal Custody: The right to make major decisions about the child's welfare, including education, healthcare, and religious upbringing.

Physical Custody: Where the child lives and the right to make day-to-day decisions about the child's care.
